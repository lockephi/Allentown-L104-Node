## 3. Key Operations

### `collapse(name: str)`
This is the central function of the engine. It takes the name of a physical or sacred constant (e.g., "speed_of_light", "GOD_CODE") and performs a full dual-layer derivation, showing how both the Thought and Physics layers converge on the measured value.

### `thought(a,b,c,d)` and `physics(intensity)`
These methods allow direct access to each of the two layers independently. `thought` calculates a value based on the sacred geometry equation, while `physics` calculates the sovereign field strength.

### `full_integrity_check()`
This runs a 12-point validation across both layers and the bridge between them. It checks for the immutability of sacred constants, the precision of physical constants, and the structural integrity of the relationships that bind the two layers.

### `predict(max_complexity, top_n)`
This is the engine's prediction mechanism. It scans for simple, low-complexity dial combinations that *do not* correspond to any known physical constant. These represent **predictions** of where new, undiscovered constants might lie. A future experimental discovery of one of these predicted values would provide strong evidence for the physical significance of the Dual-Layer Engine's model of reality.
